Introduction

Dogs are domesticated mammals from the Canidae family, distinguished by their loyalty, diverse breeds, and a long history of companionship with humans since the domestication of wolves. They are known for their keen senses of smell and hearing, various roles from working animals to family pets, and a unique ability to form a mutual emotional bond with people that benefits human well-being.


Origin Of Dogs

Dogs originated from an extinct population of gray wolves, with domestication beginning in the Old World around 27,000 to 40,000 years ago, occurring with hunter-gatherer groups before agriculture developed. Less aggressive wolves scavenged near human camps, leading to a mutualistic relationship and the gradual transformation of wolves into early dogs through generations of selection. Labradors

Labrador Retrievers are one of the most popular dog breeds worldwide, known for their friendly and outgoing nature. They are medium to large-sized dogs with a strong build, short coat, and webbed paws, making them excellent swimmers. Labradors are highly intelligent and trainable, often used as service dogs, therapy dogs, and in search-and-rescue operations. They thrive in active households and require regular exercise to stay healthy and happy.
